🔗 How to Tell if a Link Is Safe Before You Click

Posted: July 3, 2025

We’ve all received strange links — from emails, text messages, or even WhatsApp groups. But how do you know if a link is safe or a trap?

🚨 Why You Should Be Careful

Malicious links can lead to fake websites, steal your login info, or automatically download malware. Just one wrong click can compromise your phone or computer.

🧠 How to Spot a Suspicious Link

  • Check the URL: Is it misspelled? Like "faceboook.com" instead of "facebook.com"?
  • Hover Before Clicking: On computers, hover your mouse over the link to preview where it goes.
  • Too Urgent? Links that say “act fast!” or “your account will be locked” are red flags.
  • From a Stranger? Don’t trust links from unknown contacts — even if the message sounds personal.

🛡️ Free Tools to Check Any Link

  • VirusTotal — Copy & paste the link to scan it.
  • URLScan.io — Gives a visual breakdown of the site.
  • Google Safe Browsing — Checks against Google’s threat database.

✅ Final Tip

If you’re unsure, don’t click it. Ask someone knowledgeable or use the tools above. One second of doubt can save you from days of regret.
