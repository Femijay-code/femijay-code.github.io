📡 Public Wi-Fi: The Hidden Risks You Should Know

Posted: July 1, 2025

Free Wi-Fi is everywhere — cafes, airports, schools, hotels. It’s convenient, but what most people don’t realize is that **public Wi-Fi is one of the easiest ways for cyber attackers to spy on your activity.**

🔍 What's the Risk?

  • Attackers can **intercept your traffic** and steal sensitive data (emails, passwords, banking info).
  • Fake networks (called “Evil Twin” hotspots) can trick you into connecting to them.
  • Malware can be injected onto your device through insecure connections.

🛡️ How to Stay Safe on Public Wi-Fi

  • Never access banking or sensitive accounts on public networks.
  • Always verify the official name of the Wi-Fi before connecting.
  • Use a **VPN** — it encrypts your traffic and hides it from prying eyes.
  • Turn off auto-connect for Wi-Fi in your settings.
  • Use websites that start with **https://**, not just http://

✅ Tools You Can Use

  • ProtonVPN (free & secure)
  • Windscribe (good for beginners)
  • NordVPN / ExpressVPN (paid, but reliable)

Public Wi-Fi isn’t evil — but it’s dangerous **if you’re unaware**. With the right habits and tools, you can stay connected without putting your data at risk.
